Investigate the sensitivity of the solution by plotting $A$ versus $r$. How much can $r$ vary about its optimal value before the area increases $10\%$ above its minimum value?
\end{enumerate}
}

\question{tut4-q40}{Problem 40}{%
A weight $W$ is supported by two cables anchored a distance $D$ apart (see Figure P40). The cable length $L_{AB}$ is given, but the length $L_{AC}$ is to be selected. Each cable can support a maximum tension force equal to $W$. For the weight to remain stationary, the total horizontal force and total vertical force must each be zero. This principle gives the equations
\[
-T_{AB}\cos\theta + T_{AC}\cos\phi = 0,
\qquad
T_{AB}\sin\theta + T_{AC}\sin\phi = W.
\]
We can solve these equations for the tension forces $T_{AB}$ and $T_{AC}$ if we know the angles $\theta$ and $\phi$. From the law of cosines,
\[
\theta = \cos^{-1}\!\left(\frac{D^{2} + L_{AB}^{2} - L_{AC}^{2}}{2DL_{AB}}\right).
\]
From the law of sines,
\[
\phi = \sin^{-1}\!\left(\frac{L_{AB}\sin\theta}{L_{AC}}\right).
\]
For the given values $D = 6~\text{ft}$, $L_{AB} = 3~\text{ft}$, and $W = 2000~\text{lb}$, use a \texttt{while} loop in MATLAB to find $L_{AC,\min}$, the shortest length $L_{AC}$ that can be used without $T_{AB}$ or $T_{AC}$ exceeding $2000~\text{lb}$. Note that the largest $L_{AC}$ can be is $6.7~\text{ft}$ (which corresponds to $\theta = 90^\circ$). Plot the tension forces $T_{AB}$ and $T_{AC}$ on the same graph versus $L_{AC}$ for $L_{AC,\min} < L_{AC} < 6.7$.
}

\question{tut5-q19}{Problem 19*}{%
The height $h(t)$ and horizontal distance $x(t)$ traveled by a ball thrown at an angle $A$ with a speed $v$ are given by
\[
h(t) = vt\sin A - \frac{1}{2}gt^{2},
\qquad
x(t) = vt\cos A.
\]
At Earth's surface the acceleration due to gravity is $g = 9.81~\text{m/s}^{2}$.
\begin{enumerate}[label=\alph*.]
    \item Suppose the ball is thrown with a velocity $v = 20~\text{m/s}$ at an angle of $25^\circ$. Use MATLAB to compute how high the ball will go, how far it will go, and how long it will take to hit the ground.
    \item Use the values of $v$ and $A$ given in part (a) to plot the ball's trajectory; that is, plot $h$ versus $x$ for positive values of $t$.
    \item Plot the trajectories for $A = 45^\circ$ corresponding to five values of the initial velocity $v$: 20, 24, 28, 32, and 36 m/s.
\end{enumerate}
}

Obtain the surface and contour plots for the temperature. Label each axis. What is the temperature at the corner corresponding to $x = y = 0$?

\question{tut9-q44}{Problem 44}{%
The following state model describes the motion of a certain mass connected to a spring, with viscous friction on the surface, where $m = 1$, $c = 2$, and $k = 5$:
\[
\begin{bmatrix}
\dot{x}_{1} \\
\dot{x}_{2}
\end{bmatrix}
=
\begin{bmatrix}
0 & 1 \\
-5 & -2
\end{bmatrix}
\begin{bmatrix}
x_{1} \\
x_{2}
\end{bmatrix}
+
\begin{bmatrix}
0 \\
1
\end{bmatrix}
f(t).
\]
\begin{enumerate}[label=\alph*.]
    \item Use the \texttt{initial} function to plot the position $x_{1}$ of the mass if the initial position is $5$ and the initial velocity is $3$.
    \item Use the \texttt{step} function to plot the step response of the position and velocity for zero initial conditions, where the magnitude of the step input is $10$. Compare your plot with the figure shown in the tutorial sheet.
\end{enumerate}
}

\question{tut9-q45}{Problem 45}{%
Consider the following equation:
\[
5\ddot{y} + 2\dot{y} + 10y = f(t).
\]
\begin{enumerate}[label=\alph*.]
    \item Plot the free response for the initial conditions $y(0) = 10$ and $\dot{y}(0) = -5$.
    \item Plot the unit-step response for zero initial conditions.
    \item The total response to a step input is the sum of the free response and the step response. Demonstrate this fact for this equation by plotting the sum of the solutions found in parts (a) and (b) and comparing the plot with that generated by solving for the total response with $y(0) = 10$ and $\dot{y}(0) = -5$.
\end{enumerate}
}
